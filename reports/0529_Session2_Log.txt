Three reasons, in order of impact:

I didn't collect any per-pitcher prop stat distributions (K mean/std, etc.) during the context-gathering phase. That data requires separate fetches per player beyond what the game-level research covered.

A pitcher K prop needs the game context plus the prop distribution plus confirmed line and odds from a book. Any one missing field (e.g., sample_size < 5, or no confirmed line from BetMGM's typed resolver) triggers a null_data_audit downgrade, making the trace calibration-ineligible. Games only need off/def ratings + starter ERA + park factor — a smaller, more reliably sourced set.

Without it, prop lines would have had to come from web scraping alone, and mlb_daily.md explicitly says: "If a prop line is unavailable from the typed resolver or a direct sportsbook board, keep it research-only. Do not pass guessed or narrative-only lines to analyze()." Research-only traces aren't calibration-eligible, which conflicts with the session goal.
